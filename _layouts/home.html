About me

I am a third-year graduate student in MIT EECS and Mathematics, being part of Eric and Wendy Schmidt Center at Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT. I am also an affiliated researcher closely working with Prof Prof.Biwei Huang(Assistant Professor in EECS,UC San Diego) and Prof Kun Zhang(Associate Professor in CMU). Prior to leaving New York, I was fortunately supervised by Prof.Bryant Moy at NYU Center for Data Science and Prof.Michele at Grossman School of Medicine at NYU Langone. I received my master's degree jointly offered by Tandon School of Engineering and Courant Institute of Mathematics.

My research interests are focused on theory in mathematics for causality in particular probabilistic graphical models and Applied Algebraic Geometry; I develop theoretical and computational methods in Causal Representation Learning in cancer-driving mutation, genomics and other biophysical processes.
